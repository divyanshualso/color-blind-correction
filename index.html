A tool for color blinds

There are three main types of color blindness: monochromatism, dichromatism, and anomalous trichromatism. Monochromatism, or complete color blindness, is rare and results in seeing only shades of gray. Dichromatism occurs when two cone types are available, leading to difficulty distinguishing certain colors, like red and green. Anomalous trichromatism is a milder form of color blindness, where all three cone types are present but one or more are abnormal, often resulting in difficulty differentiating between red and green.
